Overview

A benchmark for the full UMI evaluation loop

Real-robot evaluation is essential for understanding whether learned manipulation policies operate reliably outside curated demonstrations. UMI-Bench focuses on the UMI-style setting, where wrist-view observation, action representation, data collection, and physical deployment are tightly coupled.

The benchmark specifies how demonstrations are collected, how scenes are reset, how policies are executed, how episodes are logged, and how task-level factors are analyzed under seen and unseen conditions.

Task Suite

Single-arm and bimanual tabletop manipulation

UMI-Bench 1.0 covers four single-arm tasks and six bimanual tasks, spanning placement precision, spatial reasoning, contact-rich interaction, dynamic scenes, sorting, long-horizon execution, and deformable manipulation.

Benchmark Protocol

Reproducible evaluation, not isolated demos

01

Data Collection

Demonstrations are captured through UMI-style wrist-view observations and deployment-compatible action interfaces.

02

Scene Reset

Initial states and task variants are specified so repeated physical trials remain comparable and auditable.

03

Policy Execution

Policies are evaluated through a consistent runner that records observations, actions, episode metadata, and outcomes.

04

Task-Factor Analysis

Results are interpreted through seen and unseen factors such as object variation, layout change, contact stability, and long-horizon progress.

Results

From aggregate scores to diagnostic task factors

UMI-Bench reports success, partial progress, subgoal completion, and factor-level changes so that policy failures can be analyzed rather than only ranked.
